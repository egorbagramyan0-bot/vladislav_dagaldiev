My speaker bio

Jensen Harris has designed and built software his entire life. In more than 15 years at Microsoft, he guided the creation of some of the most widely-used software in the world—the innovative Ribbon interface of Microsoft Office, the often-imitated redesign of Outlook, and the pioneering touch-first experience built for Microsoft Surface, which shipped on more than 200 million devices.

In 2014 he co-founded Textio and invented augmented writing, bringing AI to HR for the first time—using some of the earliest production-scale precursors of today’s LLMs, years before ChatGPT. As Textio’s first engineer, first product person, and first designer, he built the original product, then led R&D across engineering, product, design, security, and privacy. By the end of his time as CTO, hundreds of enterprises—including over a quarter of the Fortune 100—relied on Textio every day.

He went on to serve as Textio’s Chief Experience Officer and then, from 2024 to 2026, its CEO—resetting the company’s culture, reigniting product momentum, and conceiving and shipping Lavalier, an AI-native interview intelligence platform and the most ambitious software in Textio’s history. He remains a co-founder, board member, and CTO Emeritus.

Today, Jensen partners with medium and large companies to reinvent their organizations and build natively with AI, alongside building new products of his own. He coaches engineering and product leaders, facilitates T‑groups for founders, and speaks worldwide on human-computer interaction, interface design, startup leadership, and software engineering.

A graduate of Yale University and Interlochen Arts Academy with degrees in music composition, Jensen lives in Seattle with his wife, three teenage daughters, and two cats.
